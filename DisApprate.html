Play2Learn
A strategic twist on Snakes & Ladders — 16×9 board · 144 squares · 2 players · 2 pieces each · First to land both on sq 144 wins!
🎯 Objective
🏁Move both pieces from sq 1 to sq 144 before your opponent does.
🎲 Rolling & Moving
≠Different dice — move both pieces, one per die. Player freely assigns which die to which piece.
🏠Leaving home (sq 1) — need both dice even or doubles (d,d). Otherwise: both at home → turn skipped; one at home → only the free piece moves.
↩Overshoot sq 144 → piece bounces back by the excess.
1One piece finished — doubles: +d² or +d→+d in sequence; other roll: +sum, or +d₁→+d₂ / +d₂→+d₁ in sequence.
↪Sequence move — each die resolves separately; snake/ladder fires after every individual step.
🛡Shield → Red — a piece moving off a safe house onto a prime bounces back to that safe house.
🗝 Board Colours
 🐉 Red — dangerous prime (≥11) → falls per snake table
 🦅 Green — ÷5 or ÷6 → jumps to bonus square
 🛡 Blue — perfect square · safe house
ℹPrimes 2, 3, 5, 7 are not dangerous. Special falls: 131 & 139 → 49 · 11 & 13 → 1.
✦ Doubles Bonuses
🏠Doubles on sq 1 → jump piece d rows up same column. (2,2 → +2 rows · 4,4 → +4 rows)
🛡Doubles on safe house → bonus option: move piece by +d². (3,3 → +9)
=Doubles on normal square → move piece d then d in sequence (not by 2d).
🔗Immune pair + doubles → both pieces move by d² (not at home sq 1).
🔗 Immune Pair
🔗Both your pieces on the same square (not sq 1) = immune pair.
🔗Different dice + even sum → both pieces move by the full sum. (2+4 → +6 each)
🛡Immune pair moves bypass dangerous primes — no fall!
🏠No immune pair privilege when both pieces are on sq 1 (home).
⚔ Capture
⚔Land on a lone opponent piece → it goes back to 🏠 sq 1.
🛡Land on opponent piece on a safe house → you go back to 🏠.
🔗Land on opponent's immune pair → you are sent home.
🔒Sq 1 and sq 144 are always capture-free zones.
📚 Educational Mode
📚Land on a dangerous prime → a math challenge appears. Answer correctly to stay on the square instead of falling!
📊📊 Basic Math — arithmetic, algebra, geometry questions.
🎓🎓 AP Algebra — harder: functions, logs, sequences, complex numbers.
🌐Online: if either player enables edu mode (or AP level), both play with it.
🤖Computer opponent always answers correctly.

🎲Random — picks any valid move at random.
😈Mean — hunts your pieces; captures whenever possible, otherwise picks the worst move.
