Play2Learn
A strategic twist on Snakes & Ladders — 16×9 board · 144 squares · 2 players · 2 pieces each · First to land both on sq 144 wins!
🎯 Objective
🏁Move both pieces sq 1→144 before your opponent.
🎲 Rolling & Moving
≠Different dice — move both pieces (one per die). Player chooses which die goes to which piece.
=Doubles — move one piece by sum (2d) or move both pieces by sum (2d each).
🏠Leaving home (sq 1) — piece needs even sum or doubles to move out. Odd sum with both pieces home skips your turn.
↩Overshoot sq 144 → piece bounces back by the excess.
1One piece finished — doubles: +d² or +d→+d sequence; other roll: +sum, +d₁→+d₂, or +d₂→+d₁.
↪Sequence move — each die resolved separately: snake/ladder effects apply after each step.
✦ Doubles Bonuses
🏠Doubles on sq 1 → jump piece d rows up same column. (1,1→32 · 3,3→64)
🛡Doubles on safe house → option +d² move. (3,3→+9)
🗝 Board Colours
 Red — dangerous prime (≥11) → falls to nearest 🛡
 Green — ÷5 or ÷6 → jumps to bonus square
 Blue — perfect square 🛡 safe house
ℹPrimes 2, 3, 5, 7 are safe. Special: 131 & 139 → sq 49.
🔗 Immune Pair
🔗Both your pieces on the same square = immune pair — invulnerable to capture.
🔗Even sum → move both pieces by the full sum. (4+6 → +10 each)
🔗Doubles (d,d) → move both pieces by d². (3,3 → +9 each)
🛡Immune pair move — dangerous primes are completely skipped!
⚔ Capture
⚔Land on lone opponent piece → it goes back to 🏠 sq 1.
🛡Land on opponent on a safe house → you go back to 🏠.
🔗Attack an immune pair → blocked, nothing happens.
🔒Sq 1 and sq 144 are always capture-free.
📚 Educational Mode
📚Land on a dangerous prime → answer a math question to stay instead of falling!
🤖Computer always answers correctly in Educational Mode.
🤖 Computer Opponent
🤖Grandmaster — always picks the best available move.
🎲Random — picks any valid move at random.
🐌Easy — always picks the worst possible move.
⚙Select in Game Setup (after closing this screen).

🎤"Roll" → rolls the dice.
🎤"Option 1…5" / "One"…"First" → picks that move.
🎤"New game" → restarts · "Rules" → this screen.
